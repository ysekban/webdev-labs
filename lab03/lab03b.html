Welcome to Guangzhou

Guangzhou, known historically as Canton, is the third largest city in China, located in the south-eastern part of the country. With a population of over 15 million people, it's a bustling metropolis known for its modern architecture, shopping, and history.

The iconic Canton Tower stands at 600 meters and offers panoramic views of the city. It's a testament to the city's modern architectural advancements and a major tourist attraction.

The Guangzhou Opera House, designed by architect Zaha Hadid, is a masterpiece of contemporary design. Its unique structure is inspired by river valleys and the way they are transformed by erosion.

Culture and Cuisine

Guangzhou is the birthplace of Cantonese cuisine, which is one of the most famous and widely eaten cuisines in the world. The city's diverse food scene ranges from high-end restaurants to street food stalls, offering a variety of flavors to explore.

Historical Significance

With a history spanning over 2,200 years, Guangzhou is home to a number of historical sites. The Mausoleum of the Nanyue King and the Chen Clan Ancestral Hall are just a couple of the city's many historical treasures.

Trade and Economy

Being a significant port city, Guangzhou has always been a major hub for trade and commerce. It hosts the biannual Canton Fair, the largest trade fair in China, attracting businesspeople from all over the world.
